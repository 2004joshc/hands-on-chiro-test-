About Chiropractic

Chiropractic is a regulated health profession focused on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disorders of the musculoskeletal system — particularly the spine — and their effects on the nervous system and overall health.

Chiropractors use a range of hands-on techniques, most notably spinal manipulation (adjustment), to restore proper alignment, reduce pain, and improve function. The underlying principle is that proper spinal alignment allows the nervous system to communicate freely between the brain and body, supporting the body's natural ability to heal.

Yes. Chiropractic is widely recognised as one of the safest non-invasive, drug-free therapies available for neuromusculoskeletal complaints. Serious adverse events are extremely rare.

Some patients experience mild soreness or stiffness in the first 24–48 hours after an adjustment — similar to the feeling after starting a new exercise regime. Dr. Cho will always perform a thorough assessment before any treatment to ensure it is appropriate for your individual situation.

Chiropractic care is most well-known for treating:

  • Lower back pain and sciatica
  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Shoulder, knee, hip and elbow complaints
  • Sports injuries and strains
  • Posture-related pain from desk work
  • Scoliosis management
  • Pregnancy-related back pain

Beyond pain relief, many patients seek ongoing wellness care to maintain spinal health, improve posture, and support their overall wellbeing.

Most people find chiropractic adjustments comfortable and even relieving. You may hear a "popping" or "clicking" sound — this is simply gas being released from the joint, similar to cracking your knuckles, and is completely harmless.

If you are nervous or in significant pain, Dr. Cho can use gentler, low-force techniques that are just as effective without the classic adjustment sensation. Your comfort is always the priority.

Both are evidence-based health professions focused on musculoskeletal health, and there is significant overlap. The main distinctions are:

  • Chiropractic has a primary focus on spinal alignment and nervous system function, using spinal adjustments as a central tool.
  • Physiotherapy tends to focus more on rehabilitation exercises and movement re-education.

At Hands On Chiropractic, Dr. Cho combines both worlds — integrating chiropractic adjustments with therapeutic exercise and soft tissue work for comprehensive care.

Your First Visit

Your first visit typically includes:

  • A detailed health history — discussing your current concern, past injuries, lifestyle, and health goals
  • A physical examination — postural assessment, range of motion testing, orthopaedic and neurological tests
  • Diagnosis and explanation — Dr. Cho will explain what he finds in plain language
  • A personalised care plan — outlining recommended treatment and expected outcomes
  • Initial treatment — if appropriate on the same visit

Allow approximately 45–60 minutes for your first appointment.

No referral is needed. You can book directly with us by phone or in person. Chiropractors are primary contact practitioners — you don't need to see a GP first.

If you have a GP Management Plan (GPMP) or Team Care Arrangement (TCA), you may be eligible for Medicare rebates for allied health services — ask Dr. Cho or your GP about this.

Wear loose, comfortable clothing that allows easy movement — gym wear or shorts and a t-shirt are ideal. You may be asked to remove your shoes for the examination.

Please bring any relevant imaging you have (X-rays, MRI reports, or scans), and a list of current medications if applicable. Arriving 5–10 minutes early to complete a brief health form is helpful.

After your initial assessment, Dr. Cho will give you a clear recommendation with an estimated number of visits and expected progress milestones. There is no pressure to commit to extended care — your treatment is always your choice.

Treatment & Care

Dr. Cho uses a range of evidence-based techniques tailored to each patient:

  • Diversified adjustment — the classic manual spinal manipulation
  • Activator method — a low-force instrument-assisted technique, great for nervous patients or gentle care
  • Gonstead technique — specific, precise adjustments targeting individual vertebrae
  • Soft tissue therapy and myofascial release
  • Dry needling
  • Therapeutic exercise and rehabilitation
  • Lifestyle and ergonomic advice

Costs & Rebates

Yes — chiropractic is covered under extras (ancillary) cover with most private health funds in Australia, including Medibank, Bupa, HCF, NIB, and others. The rebate amount depends on your specific policy and level of cover.

We accept HICAPS in the clinic, so you can claim your rebate on the spot and only pay the gap. Bring your health fund card to each appointment.

Chiropractic is not covered under standard Medicare. However, if your GP has referred you with a Chronic Disease Management Plan (formerly EPC), you may be entitled to a partial Medicare rebate for up to 5 allied health visits per calendar year.

Speak to your GP about whether you qualify, or call our practice and we'll be happy to assist.

Yes, we accept WorkCover (workers compensation) and DVA (Department of Veterans' Affairs) patients. Please bring your claim number or DVA card to your first appointment so we can process your billing correctly.

For WorkCover, you will need a referral from your treating GP. Call us on (02) 4973 3889 if you have questions before booking.
